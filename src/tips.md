      <h2>Viral Tweet Tips</h2>
      <p>Master these fundamental strategies to create content that resonates and spreads.</p>
    </div>
    
    <div class="tips-list">
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">1</span>Hook Them in the First Line</h3>
        <p>The first line of your tweet determines whether people read the rest. Use curiosity gaps, bold statements, or surprising facts. Examples: "Most people get this wrong...", "Here's what nobody tells you...", "I tried X for 30 days..."</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">2</span>Use Threads for Complex Ideas</h3>
        <p>Threads allow you to dive deep while keeping each tweet digestible. Start with a strong hook, deliver value in each tweet, and end with a clear takeaway or call-to-action. Use (1/X) numbering for clarity.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">3</span>Include Visuals Whenever Possible</h3>
        <p>Tweets with images get 150% more retweets. Use screenshots, charts, memes, or short videos. Visual content stops the scroll and increases engagement significantly.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">4</span>Time Your Tweets Strategically</h3>
        <p>Post when your audience is most active. Generally: 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM, and 6-9 PM in your audience's timezone. Test different times and track what works for your specific followers.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">5</span>Engage Before You Post</h3>
        <p>Spend 15-30 minutes engaging with others before posting your own content. This warms up the algorithm and increases the likelihood of your tweet being seen. Reply to big accounts in your niche.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">6</span>Use the Power of Lists</h3>
        <p>People love scannable content. Use numbered lists, bullet points, and clear formatting. Examples: "7 ways to...", "5 mistakes to avoid...", "3 tools I use daily..."</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">7</span>Create Controversy (Carefully)</h3>
        <p>Contrarian takes get attention, but stay authentic. Challenge common beliefs in your niche. "Unpopular opinion:..." or "Stop doing X, do Y instead..." Just don't be controversial for no reason.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">8</span>Tell Stories, Not Just Facts</h3>
        <p>Stories create emotional connection. Share personal experiences, failures, and lessons learned. Structure: Setup → Conflict → Resolution → Takeaway. People remember stories, not statistics.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">9</span>Use Social Proof</h3>
        <p>Share results, numbers, and achievements. "Grew from 0 to 100K followers in 6 months" is more compelling than "Here's how to grow." Specificity builds credibility.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">10</span>End with a Call-to-Action</h3>
        <p>Tell people what to do next: "Retweet if you agree", "Follow for more", "Bookmark this", "What would you add?" CTAs increase engagement rates by 2-3x.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">11</span>Write Like You Speak</h3>
        <p>Conversational tone wins on Twitter. Use short sentences. Contractions are fine. Write how you'd explain something to a friend. Authenticity beats perfection.</p>
      </div>
      
      <div class="tip-card">
        <h3><span class="tip-number">12</span>Study Viral Tweets in Your Niche</h3>
        <p>Analyze what's working for others. Save tweets that perform well. Identify patterns: hooks, formatting, timing, topics. Don't copy—learn and adapt to your voice.</p>
      </div>
      
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
